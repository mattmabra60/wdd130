How to Properly Use a Knife:

  1. Hold the knife firmly with your dominant hand, gripping the handle firmly and placing your thumb and index finger on either side of the blade's base for control.
  2. Place the food item on a stable cutting surface, such as a cutting board, and position your non-dominant hand on top to hold it securely.
  3. Use a rocking motion to guide the knife blade through the food, keeping the tip of the blade in contact with the cutting surface and using a smooth, controlled motion to slice or chop.

  • Simmer: To cook food gently in liquid at a temperature just below boiling, typically for a longer period of time.

  • Bake: To cook food by surrounding it with dry heat in an oven, typically at a moderate to high temperature.
  • Grill: To cook food over direct heat, usually on a rack or grate positioned above hot coals or gas flames.
  • Fry: To cook food in hot oil or fat, typically submerged in the oil or fat until golden and crispy.
  • Broil: To cook food using high heat from above, usually in an oven or broiler, resulting in a crispy exterior.
  • Steam: To cook food by exposing it to steam, either by placing it over boiling water or using a specialized steaming appliance.
  • Whisk: To beat ingredients together rapidly using a whisk, incorporating air and creating a smooth mixture.
  • Blend: To mix ingredients together thoroughly until smooth and uniform in texture, using a blender or food processor.
  • Fold: To gently combine ingredients by carefully turning one part over another using a spatula or spoon, maintaining airiness.
  • Chop: To cut food into small, irregular pieces using a knife or other cutting tool.
  • Mince: To chop food into very small pieces, often using a knife or food processor, until finely divided.
  • Peel: To remove the outer skin or rind from fruits or vegetables using a knife or peeler.
  • Season: To add salt, pepper, herbs, spices, or other flavorings to food to enhance its taste.
  • Stir: To mix ingredients together using a spoon or utensil in a circular motion, incorporating them evenly.

Proper Cleaning Techniques for the Kitchen

  1. Clear Counterspace: Remove all food scraps, dishes, and utensils from the countertop to provide a clean and clutter-free workspace.
  2. Wash with Warm, Soapy Water: Use hot water and dish soap to wash countertops, cutting boards, and utensils, paying special attention to areas where food may have come into contact. Rinse thoroughly with clean water and dry with a clean towel.
  3. Sanitize Surfaces: Use a mixture of bleach and water or a disinfectant spray to sanitize countertops, sink, and other frequently touched surfaces. Allow the sanitizer to sit for the recommended contact time before wiping it off with a clean cloth.
